kmax extension — physical mass cap adopted
k_lim (continuity-allowed max k) rises with T_BE; hot planes inherited cold-plane kmax and were truncated, but filling to continuity gives unphysical multi-pc / hundreds-of-M⊙ "cores."
 
Cap = M_BE ≤ 41 M⊙ (= i01j07k07 = 40.98).
Surviving sensible extensions: i2j5 +4(~29), i2j4 +3(~7), i2j3 +2(~2), i3j5 +3(~14), i3j4 +2(~4), i3j3 +1(~1), i4j5 +2(~7), i1j5 +1(~40.5). Hot low-Σ j6/j7 planes already at cap — leave them.
 
Subdivision — likely NOT needed for production
For the smooth, mild Φ3 surface, the ×2 (log) grid's linear-interp error near the crossing is ~1–2% (at the grid's own noise floor), and the crossing Σ is recoverable by interpolation without a node on it. So subdivision is characterization, not necessity.
 
Decisive test (grid-spacing convergence, parallel to photon test): linearly interpolate bias to midpoint Σ=3.39e22 from i04/i05; compare to the directly-computed midpoint model. Agree within ~1–2% → don't subdivide production grid.
Recommended: a separate small "crossing strip" catalog (inserted Σ column spanning the j planes ± one neighbor) for the test + a Σ_cross(T_BE) result figure; keep production interpolation on the uniform grid. Sidesteps all the insert-numbering complexity.
Caveat: holds for Φ3; the Φ4/160 μm artifact region is more curved → its own targeted strip if quantifying the literature bias.
