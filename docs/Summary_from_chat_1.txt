2. CMF methodology project — planned scope
 
Motivation. Extension of the 2016 Men'shchikov SED-fitting paper from per-source mass uncertainties to CMF shape
recovery. Standard completeness simulations have hidden methodological flaws: (a) random-position injection
over-samples easy backgrounds; (b) extracted-source-position injection uses artificially flattened backgrounds.
Direct evidence from preliminary work: Aquila (Konyves et al. 2015) simulations had model cores brighter in the
derived surface density map by up to a factor of ~5 compared to real cores. Discrepancy was visible in the
comparison with observation-derived surface density but apparently not noticed at the time of publication.
 
Methodological framework (working concept). Simulations of CMF recovery must satisfy multiple independent
constraints simultaneously: (1) input realism (true model T-M close to recovered T-M after SED fitting bias is
accounted for); (2) range coverage (recovered cores span the full observed T-M range); (3) distributional match
to observations (after recovery, simulated cores reproduce observed properties not just in bounding region but in
internal structure). The constraint-satisfaction problem is genuinely difficult — analogous to getsf development
in requiring extensive parameter exploration. Quantitative testing required: 2D binning of T-M scatter plots,
density contours at fractional enclosure levels, ratio maps to reveal where simulated and observed populations
agree or disagree.
 
Current state of the work. Tested old BE-sphere Aquila simulation: produces apparent visual green-grey match but
on closer examination this may be an illusion driven by bias-spread of magenta input points across observation
space. Tested new model family (different density profiles): produces correct trend direction (T increasing with
M for low M, matching observations) but does not yet span full observed T-M range. Constraint satisfaction not
yet complete.
 
Diagnostic visualisation. Colour-stacked CMF: bars subdivided by mass-recovery error factor (0.7–1.4 grey/best,
1.4–2.8 green, 2.8–5.7 blue, 5.7–11 red, 11–∞ magenta), with input CMF overplotted as black curve. Visualisation
shows directly which parts of the recovered CMF are well-measured versus contaminated by mass-recovery errors.
For real observational CMFs (no ground truth), bounds plot showing systematic uncertainty envelope from
simulation-derived error distributions.
 
Publication strategy. Two-paper structure following filament template: methodology paper using Aquila as
illustration, followed by application paper extending to seven clouds. Methodology paper currently realistic
publication target; full constraint-satisfied simulation may or may not be achievable in finite time. If not,
framework paper still publishable, presenting diagnostic tools and demonstrating that no published Aquila
simulation satisfies the constraints when properly tested. Strategic framing: methodological intervention rather
than direct critique of specific published results — provides procedure future authors can adopt, lets
implications for prior work emerge from comparison.
 
Timeline and collaborator dynamics. Project to begin after current papers close out. Guoyin Zhang capable of
strong technical execution but planning and conceptual decisions remain Men'shchikov's responsibility. Recently
joined ALMA AVALANCHE consortium (Motte-led IMF study) which may slow CMF project pace; this is acceptable and
possibly beneficial — slower pace allows reflective methodological work that daily-communication mode has not.
Men'shchikov as external expert on AVALANCHE provides useful access without consortium obligations. CMF paper
conversation with André Men'shchikov to be deferred ~1 year, until tested approach and results are concrete;
brief professional notification rather than draft sharing or consultation.
 
3. Diagnostic visualisation framework
 
Colour-stacked CMF (for simulations with known ground truth). Each CMF bin is a stacked bar with colours
indicating per-source mass recovery accuracy. Error factor bands: 0.7–1.4 (best), 1.4–2.8, 2.8–5.7, 5.7–11, 11–∞.
Stacking from bottom (best accuracy) to top. Input model CMF overplotted as separate curve. Pairs CMF-from-SD and
CMF-from-SED plots reveal differential performance of mass derivation methods. Old Aquila: substantial high-error
contamination across mass range, recovered CMF span much wider than input. New simulation: narrower recovered
CMF, dominated by green/blue bands.
 
Bounded CMF (for real observational data). Standard CMF with systematic uncertainty band derived from per-source
error distributions calibrated by simulation. Per-source error distributions conditional on local background
properties. Monte Carlo sampling preserving correlations between systematic and stochastic components. Bounds
typically asymmetric (mass underestimation more common than overestimation) and wider at low mass. Bounds
expected to be substantially wider than published statistical error bars, demonstrating that systematic
uncertainty dominates.
 
T-M comparison (three populations). Grey: real observed core values from SED fitting. Green: SED values from
running fitting on simulated extraction. Magenta: true input model values. Three pairwise comparisons: green-grey
shows whether simulation reproduces observations after recovery; green-magenta shows SED-fitting bias (decoupled
from extraction issues); grey-magenta would show what observers see versus underlying truth. The full bias chain
is magenta → green → grey.
 
Quantitative tests. 2D binning in log-space; histogram2d with bins chosen for ~10 points per occupied bin;
contours at fractional enclosure levels (25%, 50%, 75%) for cross-population comparison; ratio maps (population A
/ population B, normalised) showing where distributions agree and disagree. Three-panel figure: scatter,
contours, ratio map. Python code prepared (uses numpy.histogram2d, matplotlib.contour, pcolormesh; saves PDF and
PNG).
 
4. Strategic considerations
 
Working arrangement. Men'shchikov's institutional independence at IPAG/CEA permits sustained methodological work
that group-based researchers cannot undertake. Six solo papers 2013–2023 established the line. Collaboration with
Zhang since 2024 doubles technical capacity while preserving conceptual independence; tandem mode (Men'shchikov
directs, Zhang executes) suits both partners. Research programme self-defined and not subject to consortium
negotiations.
 
Long-term programme. Coherent multi-paper line: 2016 SED-fitting paper, hires algorithm papers, getsf method
papers, 2023 resolvedness paper, current filament fitting trio (methods + application + Nature), prospective CMF
methodology and application papers. Pattern: methodological scrutiny of how observational pipelines extract
physical quantities from Herschel data. By the time CMF papers appear, the programme will have an established
intellectual identity that helps individual papers land effectively. Methodologically-grounded scepticism is an
underprovisioned position in the field.
 
Framing. Methodology papers more likely to be adopted than critical papers. Constructive framing ("here is the
systematic uncertainty budget") attracts citation; confrontational framing ("your results are wrong") triggers
defensive reaction. The 2016 paper's relatively low citation rate suggests confrontational framing was
suboptimal. CMF paper to be framed as quantitative bias budget that can be applied to any future measurement,
with implications for past work emerging from honest application rather than direct charge.
 
Field dynamics. ALMA-IMF tension currently active, providing context for CMF methodology work. Time window
favourable for methodological intervention. Zhang's AVALANCHE involvement creates internal-pipeline access for
the methodology results. Direct revisit of Konyves et al. 2015 unavoidable given the contrast factor of 5;
framing as application of new framework rather than critique of original work; Men'shchikov was co-author of 2015
paper but did not control analysis decisions, providing legitimate standing for revisit.
 
Pacing. Two-year pressure-free window after current papers close out. Methodological work benefits from slower
pace; Zhang's reduced availability via AVALANCHE supports rather than threatens this. CMF project may take 18
months to several years; this is acceptable. Men'shchikov age 66, multiple years of productive work expected;
project sized to that horizon rather than to immediate output pressure.
