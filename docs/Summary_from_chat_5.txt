Summary for continuation — chat 5
Continuation handoff for Alexander Men'shchikov's RT-based correction of Herschel
SED-derived prestellar core masses (Aquila, d=260 pc). This chat focused on three
things: a known-truth injection test, a full verification of the model-grid catalog
(diagnosing the 2.4e22 anomaly), and the per-band noise / band-selection model that
ties everything to the Könyves (2015) cirrus and completeness model. All results were
folded into the paper draft.

1. Known-truth injection test (18 injected cores)
One critical BE model (M_BE=0.080 Msun, mass-averaged T_avg=11.98 K) injected at 18
positions in the real Aquila field, recovered through the full chain.

Measurement is unbiased when the column is right: the injected MODEL column,
footprint-integrated with local bg subtraction, returns 0.98 M_BE (median; 16–84%
0.92–1.04).
Recovered TEMPERATURES are warm-biased: per-pixel SED fit on bg-subtracted bands
gives median 13.8 K vs truth 11.98 K → +1.8 K, roughly uniform across the 18,
only weakly correlated with local cloud column (which spans a narrow 2.5–4.4e21).
Implied mass underestimate ~0.5–0.6 M_BE from temperature alone.
Cause = separation residual, not the temperature machinery. Cores are isolated
and the smoothing is faithful (smoothed map == per-pixel fit inside footprints), so
the bias is in the per-band getsf source/background separation leaving warm cloud
residual under the cores. Clean (true-field) separation → bg-sub colour T = 11.97 K,
mass 0.92 M_BE. So separation quality is the dominant remaining error, larger
than the ~10% the RT grid is meant to absorb. Next lever = background under the
source (lower-envelope / getimages-style bg; per-source deblended images).

Drafted as §Validation "Known-truth injection test" (sec:injtest).

2. Catalog verification — the 2.4e22 block diagnosed (NOT a bug)
Two files in /mnt/project: bes_model_params_catalog (2.4e22 recomputed) and
bes_model_params_catalog_cSD_04-bad (old). Loader: fit_mass.load_catalog + COLS.

Diff: the two files are identical except the 2.4e22 block; the recompute changed
those 45 models by only ~1% (photon-noise level) → reproduced the same values, did
NOT fix them. Deterministic ⇒ not a transient glitch.
Cause = 160 µm zero-crossing in the 4-BAND SED fit only. IC160bs block medians
cross zero with column: +23.8, +9.8, +2.76, +0.40 @2.4e22, −0.94, −1.64. The
with-160/without-160 fit disagreement |T4bs−T3bs| spikes to 0.81 K at 2.4e22 (~0
elsewhere). The 42 broken models have IC160 near zero (−2.9..+0.8): a small residual
160 incompatible with the cold SED collapses the fit to T_SED≈5 K, mass up to ×9. The
3 survivors have strongly-negative IC160 (−3.5..−7.8) → dropped as non-detections,
exactly as 4.8e22/9.6e22 (clean) already do.
3-band fit (250/350/500) is clean in ALL blocks: Chi2≈0, 0 flagged; smooth
monotonic M_SED3bs/M_BE = 0.85, 0.87, 0.90, 0.91, 0.96, 1.03. The ~15 4-band-flagged
models in other blocks are all clean in 3-band (same 160 effect).
Td_avg/Td_emb, IC intensities, and analytic BE columns (M_BE, R_BE, rho_BE, T_BE) are
smooth/finite/monotonic across all blocks.
Verdict: do NOT recompute any other block. Only 2.4e22 is affected, and the fix is
band selection, not recomputation.

Drafted as §Method "SED band selection and cirrus confusion noise" (sec:bandselect).

3. Why the gate failed, and the noise model that fixes it

Root cause of the gate failure: the model catalog is noiseless, so a placeholder
per-band error FXT_ERR = 1e-10 was used "to allow fitting." That makes S/N ≈
flux/1e-10 huge for every band, so the existing S/N > 1 band-inclusion gate never
fires and the near-zero 160 stays in the fit. Adding a calibration term (c·F)²
does NOT fix it (with σ_noise=1e-10 it makes S/N = 1/c ≈ 10 for every band; still
never drops 160). Calibration belongs in the fit weighting (interpretable Chi²),
not in the gate.
Fix = realistic per-band noise. Herschel is confusion-limited, not
instrument-limited, so the gate noise is the cirrus fluctuation (Könyves 2015):

σ_N = 3.9e20 · (N_back/7e21)^1.6 ;  f ≡ σ_N/N_back  (N_back = SD_emb).
f per block: 3.4, 5.1, 7.7, 11.7, 17.7, 26.8 %.
I_λ,back from the interior-plateau cloud temperature T_back (exclude the warm
ISRF rim) via O&H β=2 (κ=83.364·(100/λ)² cm²/g dust), μ=2.8, dust-to-gas=0.01, MJy/sr.
σ_λ = sqrt(σ_instr,λ² + (f·I_λ,back)²) ; gate: I_λ,bs / σ_λ > 1.


Result: keeps 160 at low columns (S/N 10→2.1), drops it from 2.4e22 up (S/N 0.4)
→ all 45 @2.4e22 fall onto the clean 3-band masses (0.91). Robust: the crossing flux
is small and S/N is far from 1 on both sides, so I_back normalization uncertainty
(~1.5×) moves nothing across the threshold.

Settled parameters (user-confirmed): gate threshold k=1 (tried 3, 2, settled 1);
smoothing kernel = plain Gaussian tied to getsf maxsize=80″; S/N gate uses the
minimized 3-band S/N min_λ(S_λ/F_flat_λ) over 250/350/500.
Interior T_back per block (K), provided by user for SD_emb = 3e21, 6e21, 1.2e22,
2.4e22, 4.8e22, 9.6e22: 13.9, 11.6, 9.74, 8.47, 7.67, 7.02. Catalog intensities are
in MJy/sr.
Rim subtlety (resolved): single-interior-T analytic I_back underestimates (e.g. 77
vs the user's measured 119 at 250 µm) because every sightline to the interior also
integrates the warm rim — a 2-layer LOS (~20% of the column at a 20 K rim) lifts I250
from 77→118 and lifts I160 far more (rim dominates at 160). For the GATE the simple
T_back estimate is adequate (decision is binary, wide margin); use the measured
per-band cloud intensities only if the high-column completeness boundary needs to be
precise. (A 146 vs 119 discrepancy the user raised turned out to be a 160-vs-250 mixup,
not a resampling bug — resampling is fine, surface brightness conserved.)

4. Könyves (2015) Appendix B.2 completeness — the grid reproduces it (validation)
PDF of the relevant pages is in /mnt/project: 2015_Konyves_etal_aa2586115_p3033.pdf.
Their apparent column-density significance:
S̃ = 1.5 · [1/(1+(θ/R_BE)²)] · [B350(T_core)/B350(T_back)] · 18·(N_back/7e21)^−0.6
(intrinsic BE contrast 1.5; beam dilution, θ = column-density-map HPBW = 18.2″;
temperature dilution at a 350 µm fiducial; cirrus factor = 1/f). ≥90% complete at S̃>5.
Evaluated on our catalog (T_core = Td_avg, T_back = interior values):

Completeness mass: ~0.02 Msun @3e21 → ~6 Msun @4.8e22 → ~18 Msun @9.6e22.
Survivor sets MATCH our independent per-band gate: 14/31 @4.8e22 (gate) == 14/31
(S̃>5); 3/24 vs 2/24 @9.6e22. Two unrelated constructions, same boundary.
The temperature-dilution term is the SAME LOS-temperature physics as our mass bias, so
the grid also reproduces Könyves B.2a/b: derived/true mass ~0.8, SED T high ~0.8–1 K,
against our 3-band M_SED/M_BE = 0.83–0.91.

⇒ The grid reproduces both the published completeness curve and the published bias —
external validation as the baseline for the RT correction. Drafted as §Validation
"Completeness and the HGBS confusion model" (sec:completeness, eq:signif).
Two thresholds, one cirrus law: per-band S/N>1 = SED band inclusion (the 160 fix);
S̃>5 = source detection / completeness.
