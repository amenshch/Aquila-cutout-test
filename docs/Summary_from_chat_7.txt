Session summary

Corrector development (validated). The correction now folds in the Könyves environment-dependent flux loss via a
recoverable-mass grid (footprint = min(η·H_n, r_noise), pedestal subtracted), plus two shape axes measured
identically in grid and getsf: the footprint/FWHM concentration and the source FWHM, both from the 161 µm
surfdens band. The 4D corrector (recoverable mass, Σ, concentration, FWHM) recovers the injected masses
accurately where the grid covers the sources: cSD_01 = 0.99, cSD_02 = 1.02, with the FWHM axis breaking the
recoverable-mass fold that caused earlier low-mass overshoot.

Confirmed unmeasurability. cSD_04 (2.4×10²²) and cSD_05 (4.8×10²²) are genuinely undetectable — 0/78 and 0/57
injection matches, median peak surfdens S/N of 3.6 and 2.0, both below 5σ. This is the completeness limit M_lim ∝
Σ² manifesting, not a bug.

The central finding — geometry mismatch. Comparing model surfdens FWHM (convolved to the catalogue's 18.2″)
against 685 real Aquila cores: critical BES matches observed sizes below ~1 M☉ but grows too large above it, and
22% of the grid (high-mass corner, up to 600″) occupies a size regime with no observed counterpart (real cores
cap at ~110″). Critical BE spheres are the wrong template for massive cores; pressure-truncated (non-critical)
BES with ξ_max < 6.451 as a new grid axis is the remedy. The 1.5×beam size floor in the reference extraction
means the required truncation is, if anything, stronger than the direct ratios show.

Paper updated. US spelling restored, two lost figures (gridconv, fit_validation) recovered from the 260612
version, and three new pieces added this session: the recoverable-mass subsection, the concentration observable,
the completeness-limit figure/equation, and now the geometry subsection documenting the size mismatch and the
pressure-truncation path forward.

Files for the project baseline: rt_mass_correction.tex, fit_mass.py (4D RecoverableCorrector),
add_recoverable_mass.py, bes_model_params_catalog_rec, and the plots.
